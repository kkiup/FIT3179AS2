Shark Attack in U.S.

With the increase in water recreation in recent years, there seems to be an increase in shark attacks, but is this really the case? This dataset file contains shark attacks from around the world, and I have chosen only the United States as the subject of my study.

Why Florida?

Using the two maps on the right, we can see that Florida has the highest frequency of shark attacks, and although shark attacks have occurred in other coastal states, they have all differed greatly from Florida in terms of numbers. Gavin Naylor, who manages ISAF said the strong tidal flow in the Ponce de Leon Inlet leads to more baitfish in the area, which in turn boosts shark numbers. And outside the inlet, on the Atlantic side, there is a patch of water good for surfers. The sharks are provoked when people splash their hands and kick their feet.

Are sharks more aggressive?

The graph on the right shows a clear increasing trend in the number of shark attacks. One might think that this is due to the increased aggressiveness of the sharks, however, we should also consider the dissemination of information. In the past, people could only convey n ews with the help of newspapers and magazines, but nowadays, the advent of computers has made it easier to keep statistics. The graph on the left shows that sharks are far less of a threat than people think.

Why Shark Attack?

The pie chart on the far right shows that most of the time the shark just attacks people for no reason, perhaps it is just trying to check if the object in front of it is edible by its teeth. Through the chart "What Was Victim Doing", We can see that these behaviours can easily be misunderstood by the shark, and perhaps the shark sees them as provocative.
